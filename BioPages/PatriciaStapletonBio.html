Patricia Stapleton is a comparative political science and public policy scholar. She holds a PhD and MPhil in Political Science from the CUNY Graduate Center, as well as an MA in French Literature from Rutgers University. Her research interests include the regulation of biotechnology, both in food production and assisted reproductive technologies, with a focus on risk assessment, management, and communication. In addition to her work on science and technology policy, she has contributed to pedagogical development in political science, which WPI recognized with the 2017 Moruzzi Young Faculty Award for Innovation in Undergraduate Education. Dr. Stapleton employs active learning techniques to keep students engaged and to meet course learning objectives.
